Conversion Funnel Redesign

Changing how women invest in their goals
View Project

After shipping the MVP of Ellevest, a goal-based digital investment platform for women, I conducted a user research campaign to gather initial product feedback. Patterns began to emerge in the users' pain points, especially during the conversion funnel. This included confusion around multiple calls to action on the financial plan, uncertainty about the account opening process, and accidental duplicate deposits.

I was inspired by the fact that Ellevest isn't your traditional investment advisor—instead of picking stocks to beat the market, its mission is to help women reach their financial goals and the brand experience is more like a lifestyle company than a financial services firm. Ethnography research had found that our user thinks of her financial life holistically, making trade-offs between goals. I began to think of her goals as discrete objects that could be funded separately, similar to a shopping cart experience, so I looked to e-commerce checkout flows for inspiration.

I began my design explorations with wireframing and tested variations of the conversion flow. I iterated on the most successful prototype incorporating feedback, then collaborated with Product and Engineering through development. The updated flow launched in the fall of 2016.
